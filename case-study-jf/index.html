Amazon Chime Mobile

Meeting join flow redesign · UX team of 2
AWS · Fall 2021-Spring 2022

Background

In 2021 the Amazon Chime design team completed a full redesign of our desktop and web clients with a fresh new UI, consolidated codebase, and sweeping accessibility improvements for users. Mobile clients followed shortly after, with general release in October 2022 [link to app store].

The WHY

My part in this initiative was to match the desktop app’s new and improved UX for joining meetings — a preview modal with options to configure audio and video settings.

In addition to camera/microphone checks, the desktop “green room” modal provides a liminal space for meeting participants to fix their hair and gather their thoughts before dropping in. This moment would require special consideration for mobile users — how might we lower friction AND match the confidence they feel joining a meeting from their desk at home?

  • Significant percentage of mobile users join with dial in (x%), and anecdotally we know mobile dial in is a solid backup option for desktop users experiencing wifi issues

Safe assumptions:

  • Likely multitasking (walking, driving, caring for children)
  • Likely in a noisy environment (outdoors, at the grocery store, on public transit)
  • Likely distracted by all of the above (see also: running late for their meeting)

Accessibility SME review: [clickbait headline/small image]

A slider component may have some value for sighted users who are on the go/concerned about accidentally answering a call in their pocket. But for users who don’t navigate visually and/or don’t use the touch screen, a better experience would be focusable buttons. Programmatically, we could provide both by turning the slider background into tappable regions.

Stakeholder input (final decision): [click bait headline/small image]

Through a series of feedback sessions with stakeholders, it became clear that building a custom slider component would put engineers at risk of missing their deadline. Native button components would be straightforward to implement, and could be styled quickly to match the new visual design.
